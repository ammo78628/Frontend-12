##What are the various formatting tags in HTML?

Formatting tags are used to change the visual appearance or the semantic meaning of text without using CSS. They help the browser and screen readers understand the importance of specific words.

<b> and <strong>: Both make text bold, but <strong> indicates that the text is of high importance.

<i> and <em>: Both italicize text, but <em> (emphasized) indicates stress emphasis.

<mark>: Highlights text (usually with a yellow background).

<small>: Renders text in a smaller font size.

<sub> and <sup>: Defines subscript (below the line) and superscript (above the line) text.

<del> and <ins>: Shows "deleted" text (with a strikethrough) and "inserted" text (underlined).

Example:
```
<strong>Warning:</strong> This action is <em>permanent</em>.

The chemical formula for water is H<sub>2</sub>O.
```
